Cold Reading

A technique where a person (the "reader") gathers information about another person (the "subject") without prior knowledge. They use observation, general statements, and the subject's reactions to guide the conversation and create the illusion of insight.

Rainbow Reading

This is a specific type of cold reading technique. It involves making statements that simultaneously offer a trait and its opposite. This allows the reader to "cover all possibilities" and increase the likelihood that the subject will find something in the statement that resonates with them. -# Example: You are a very kind and considerate person, but when somebody does something to break your trust, you feel deep-seated anger.

Hot Reading

This involves the reader having prior knowledge about the subject. This knowledge can be gathered through research, observation of the subject beforehand, or even through hidden cues during the reading. -# Example: Reader might discreetly observe a piece of jewelry the subject is wearing and then "intuitively" mention it during the reading.
Maintain a healthy dose of skepticism.

So... What makes for a GOOD Reading?

Validation & Confirmation: Subject feels strong sense of validation and confirmation from the reading, even if the information isn't always easy to hear. The reading may offer a new perspective on their life and provide a sense of guidance or clarity. Subjective information being turned objective and well-defined: Unlike rainbow reading, subjective interpretations from reading a subject that yields objective information is key, like describing the meaning of the appearance of a certain aura color and making well-defined objective & binary conclusions from it. Bonus: Uncommon Events & Personal Details like Names: Describing specific past events or experiences that are highly personal and unlikely to be known to the general public. Types of Meditations (part 1)

-# There are other forms of meditations that aren't included here. Zen Meditation
(Zen Buddhism) This type of meditation emphasizes mindfulness and being present in the moment. It encourages letting go of thoughts and judgement, cultivating a state of awareness and tranquility. Let thoughts flow down the river. This is not the same as trying to force all thoughts away. Observe thoughts and sensations as they arise and pass. The goal of Zen mind meditation is not to achieve a specific state or attain enlightenment, but rather to cultivate a deeper understanding of oneself and the nature of reality. It is a continuous practice that requires patience, discipline, and a willingness to let go of expectations. (It has a strong emphasis on Zazen, a specific seated posture without focusing on a particular object.) Zen meditation utilizes koans, paradoxical riddles, to challenge the mind and break through habitual thinking patterns.
Walking Meditation
(Kinhin) A form of moving meditation that integrates mindfulness into everyday activities.
